05FAQ

Questions,
answered straight.

What does NBL Consulting actually do?
Four things, separately or together: paid media (Google, Meta, TikTok, programmatic, and specialist networks), SEO (technical audits through ongoing management), content (editorial strategy and production in English and Chinese), and strategy (go-to-market, market entry, fractional leadership). The common thread is that we deliver the work, not just the recommendations.
Which industries do you work with?
We're industry-agnostic by design. Recent work spans consumer platforms, finance and trading, media, and regulated industries. The mechanics of growth — acquisition, search visibility, content that converts — transfer across sectors; what changes is the channel mix and the compliance constraints, and we've worked under strict ones.
How do engagements work?
Two common shapes. A scoped project: an audit, a launch, a campaign build — fixed deliverable, fixed timeline. Or an ongoing engagement: we operate a channel (or several) month to month, embedded with your team. Most clients start with a scoped project so both sides can judge the fit before committing.
Do you only work with Hong Kong businesses?
No. Hong Kong is home and we work in English and Chinese, but our clients and their markets span the region and beyond. Campaign work in particular is location-independent — we run programmes targeting markets we've never had an office in, because the data doesn't care where the desk is.
Why choose a small team over a big agency?
Because the people you meet are the people who do the work. Big agencies sell you the senior team and staff you with the junior one. We don't have a junior one. You also get honesty that's hard to find on retainer: if a channel isn't working, we say so and move the budget — our model is aligned with your results, not with billable scope.
Can you produce content in Chinese?
Yes — written, not machine-translated. We work in English, Cantonese, and Mandarin, which matters for Hong Kong and regional audiences where tone and register are easy to get wrong from the outside.
